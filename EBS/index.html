Product piracy: Here, counterfeiters attempt to use a well-known company’s logo and brand name on their goods or clothing. India offers knockoff versions of well-known brands. The faker aims to profit off the well-deserved reputation of a well-known brand.

Trade dressing: With the exception of a few adjustments made by the counterfeiter, the fashion, style, and design of a competitor’s goods are brazenly reproduced.

Tech Leapfrogging: It takes time for a well-known brand to gain a following, and there are several methods for analyzing the consumer market. if a competing brand waits until the dominant business has established itself before entering the market. Cloning: This technique entails using an original product and a big-name brand that has been stolen. Clones can more easily sell at reduced prices if there are no patents in force or if trademarks and copyrights are not present.
